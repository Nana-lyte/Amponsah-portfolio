About me

A little about
my background

I grew up asking too many questions and somehow turned that into a career. My research sits at the intersection of cognitive science, computational modeling, and decision theory — I'm interested in how humans form beliefs, update them, and occasionally get things very wrong.

"The goal isn't to make machines think like humans — it's to understand what thinking actually means."

Before starting my PhD, I studied psychology and computer science at NYU, where I worked on a longitudinal study of memory consolidation during sleep. Outside the lab, I'm a volunteer math tutor, an enthusiastic home cook, and a very slow but committed trail runner.

I believe good research should be open and accessible. Most of my work is freely available, and I try to write about it in plain language on this site.
